Fairness Impossibility: Chouldechova's Theorem
Theorem (Chouldechova 2017). Let hat Y be a binary predictor and A in 0, 1 a protected attribute. If base rates differ across groups (P(Y=1 mid A=0) is not equal to P(Y=1 mid A=1)), then it is impossible to simultaneously satisfy:
Calibration within groups: P(Y=1 mid hat Y =s, A=a) = s for all s, a
Equal false positive rates: P( hat Y =1 mid Y=0, A=0) = P( hat Y =1 mid Y=0, A=1)
Equal false negative rates: P( hat Y =0 mid Y=1, A=0) = P( hat Y =0 mid Y=1, A=1)
Normative implication. This is not a technical gap to be engineered around — it is a mathematical identity. Choosing which criterion to satisfy is a normative decision. Calibration (condition 1) ensures that a score of 0.7 means "70% likely to recidivate" equally across groups, which is often required for epistemic defensibility. Equal FPR/FNR (conditions 2–3) impose symmetric error burdens, which is often required on equity grounds. These goals conflict whenever base rates differ, and the conflict cannot be resolved by a better algorithm. stratum-eval's fairness compatibility checker surfaces which pair of criteria the submitted model satisfies and which it cannot, making the normative tradeoff explicit rather than hiding it in a single aggregate metric.
